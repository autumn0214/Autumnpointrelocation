Residency & Visa Pathways

Costa Rica offers a range of temporary and permanent residency visas for expats and investors:

  • Pensionado (Retiree) Visa: Proof of lifetime pension/income ($1,000 USD monthly)
  • Rentista (Passive Income) Visa: Proof of $2,500 monthly income for 2 years or $60,000 deposit in a Costa Rican bank
  • Inversionista (Investor) Visa: Investment in Costa Rica, typically $150,000+ USD into property or business
  • Permanent Residency: Eligibility after 3 years of temporary residency or family ties (spouse/child are citizens)
  • Work Permit: Obtained if a Costa Rican company sponsors specialized foreign workers
  • Student Visa: For international students enrolled in recognized institutions
  • Marriage/Family Visa: Available to spouses, children, or immediate relatives of Costa Rican citizens/residents
  • Refugee/Asylee Status: Humanitarian protection for those meeting specific criteria
  • International/Remote Worker Visa: For those working online for companies outside Costa Rica (limited, new program)

Popular Expat Cities & Regions

San José / Central Valley: The urban hub, offering the best healthcare, international schools, modern amenities, and cooler climates.

Tamarindo / Guanacaste (Pacific): Famous for its stunning beaches, surf culture, and a huge, active North American expat community. Hot and dry climate.

Dominical / Southern Pacific: Known for its lush rainforests, intense biodiversity, and a laid-back, eco-conscious lifestyle. Higher rainfall and more remote.

Cost of Living Estimate (Couple)

$2,500 - $4,500 USD / month

  • High End: Living in a modern condo in Escazú or beachfront on the Gold Coast.
  • Low End: Renting a local-style home (Tico-house) outside the major expat centers.
  • Note: Prices for imported goods and gasoline can be high. Local produce and services are affordable.
